# Product Marketing Context
## Men's Outdoor Worker Sun Protection

**Status**: Early validation — niche discovery phase  
**Date**: 2026-05-05  
**Business model**: Dropshipping (test first, scale on product-market fit)  
**Founder note**: Founder is 28, works outdoors in Miami, and personally experiences this problem. First-person niche.

---

## 1. Niche Definition

**Niche**: Simplified, men-first sun protection for outdoor workers in hot, humid climates  
**Hero product category**: All-in-one SPF moisturizer or sunscreen stick — portable, sweat-resistant, 30-second application, no routine required

This is not a single SKU yet. The "product" at this stage is the **niche and solution category**: frictionless daily sun protection designed specifically for men who work outdoors, not men who choose to be outdoors recreationally.

The business opportunity is not product invention — it is **curation and positioning**. The products exist. The branding, education, and identity fit do not.

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary Customer
- **Gender**: Men (primary); women with the same outdoor-worker lifestyle (secondary, open)
- **Age**: 20–35
- **Occupation**: Outdoor blue-collar and active outdoor roles — construction, landscaping, delivery, outdoor retail, trades, warehouse
- **Climate**: Hot, humid, year-round sun exposure — Miami-type markets
- **Routine status**: No existing skincare routine whatsoever
- **Knowledge level**: Low — does not understand SPF, daily vs. occasional protection, or application basics

### Psychographic Profile
- Physically active by necessity, not by choice
- Health-aware but not health-obsessed; starting to notice the visible consequences of sun damage
- Skeptical of "beauty" or "skincare" framing — would respond to performance, protection, and utility framing instead
- Pragmatic buyer; needs the *why* explained simply before they'll try something new
- Values simplicity and speed — no time or interest in rituals

### Moment of Awareness (Purchase Trigger)
The customer is typically in their late 20s or early 30s and has begun noticing visible skin changes — uneven tone, early lines, roughening texture. This is a **high-intent, low-knowledge** buyer: motivated to act but has no roadmap. The product and brand need to meet them at this exact moment.

---

## 3. Pain Points

### Primary
1. **Complexity barrier**: Existing routines feel designed for women or people with time. A man on a job site at 7am will not do a 5-step routine.
2. **Identity mismatch**: Sunscreen and skincare branding is overwhelmingly feminine or aesthetics-focused. This customer does not see himself in the product or the customer.
3. **Knowledge gap**: Does not know what SPF means, why daily protection differs from beach protection, or how to apply correctly. Education is part of the product, not an afterthought.
4. **Format friction**: Most sunscreens leave white cast, greasiness, or residue that is incompatible with a work environment. The product must feel like nothing.
5. **Habit gap**: No existing routine means no natural trigger for reorder. The barrier to first purchase is also the barrier to consistency.

### Secondary
- Products are not marketed where this person shops or in language they respond to
- No one has clearly communicated the compounding, irreversible cost of daily unprotected sun exposure to this customer

---

## 4. Value Proposition

### Draft Positioning Statement
> Sun protection built for men who work outside. One product. Thirty seconds. No routine required.

### Core Promise
You don't need to become a skincare person. You just need one thing that works, fits in your pocket, and takes less time than washing your hands.

### Why This Is Differentiated
| Existing option | What they do | What they miss |
|---|---|---|
| Neutrogena Sport, Banana Boat Sport | Outdoor/sport sunscreen | Positioned for recreation and beach — not daily occupational use. No identity fit for workers. |
| Jack Black, Bulldog, Every Man Jack | Men's skincare | Assumes existing awareness and routine. Not built for first-time buyers. |
| Raw Elements, Stream2Sea | Mineral/reef-safe | Eco-focused niche, premium price, wrong identity |
| O'Keeffe's, WorkingHands | Occupational skincare | Hand and body repair — not sun protection |

**The gap**: No brand sits at the intersection of men + outdoor workers + zero-routine buyers + occupational daily sun exposure + education-first positioning.

---

## 5. Brand Voice

- **Tone**: Direct, no-nonsense, practical. Not clinical. Not aspirational.
- **Language**: Plain English. No skincare jargon. Explain terms when used.
- **Frame**: Utility product, not beauty product. This does a job. It fits your life. It's built for how you actually work.
- **Avoid**: "Glow," "radiant," "ritual," "routine," "skin goals," "self-care"
- **Use**: "Protect," "damage," "simple," "daily," "works," "takes 30 seconds"

---

## 6. Go-to-Market (Early Signals — Not Finalized)

**Channels to explore**:
- Instagram/TikTok content targeting outdoor workers — sun damage education, "what to use and why" in under 60 seconds
- Facebook/Instagram ads with occupation targeting (construction, landscaping, delivery)
- **Spanish-language content**: **Primary channel** for Miami and South Florida markets. The outdoor labor market in this region is heavily Spanish-speaking and is almost entirely untouched by skincare brands in that language. This is a first-mover advantage — treat it as equal priority to English content, not a secondary add-on.
- Physical distribution (long-term): hardware stores, gas stations, job site convenience

> **⚠ FLAG FOR LATER**: Specific channel strategy, ad creative, keyword research, and conversion funnel belong in Sales Enablement and Growth frameworks — not here.

## 7a. Supplier Research Framework

**Status**: Not started — complete before selecting a test SKU.

### Platforms to Search
| Platform | Notes |
|---|---|
| **CJ Dropshipping** | Best first stop — large catalog, US warehouse options, faster shipping than AliExpress |
| **Zendrop** | US-based, faster fulfillment, better for branded/white-label; check MOQ requirements |
| **AliExpress** | Widest raw selection; use for discovery and benchmarking, not primary supplier |

### Product Specs to Prioritize
These are non-negotiable filters. Reject any candidate that fails more than one.

| Spec | Requirement | Why |
|---|---|---|
| SPF rating | SPF 50+ | Below 50 is underselling the protection benefit; hard to market confidently |
| Sweat resistance | Labeled sweat- or water-resistant (40 or 80 min) | Core use case is active outdoor work — must hold up |
| Finish/feel | No white cast; lightweight, non-greasy | Format friction is a primary pain point — product must feel like nothing |
| Format | Stick, lotion, or balm; pocket-sized; no pump bottles | Portability is part of the value prop; must fit in a work pants pocket |
| Ingredients | Mineral (zinc oxide) preferred; chemical acceptable if no cast | Mineral tends to leave white cast — verify finish before accepting |

### Unit Economics Targets
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Cost per unit (landed) | $3–$8 |
| Target retail price | $18–$28 |
| Gross margin target | ~65–75% at midpoint retail ($23) |
| MOQ for test | 1 unit (dropship) or ≤50 units (sample run) |

At $5 landed / $23 retail, margin is ~78% before platform fees and ads. At $8 landed / $18 retail, margin is ~56% — still viable but tighter. Reject any unit cost above $10 unless retail can support $30+.

### Evaluation Checklist (Per Candidate Product)
- [ ] Meets SPF 50+ and sweat-resistance claims (check label copy, not just listing title)
- [ ] Finish confirmed no white cast (order sample before committing)
- [ ] Format is portable — stick or slim tube preferred
- [ ] Supplier ships from US warehouse or quotes ≤10 day delivery to Miami
- [ ] Listing has ≥4.5 stars with ≥50 reviews (AliExpress/CJ) or supplier provides product data sheet
- [ ] Landed unit cost fits $3–$8 window
- [ ] White-label or unbranded packaging available (for testing without trademark issues)

### Output of This Phase
Select 2–3 candidate SKUs to order samples. Evaluate finish, packaging, and feel in-hand before finalizing. Feed supplier terms (lead time, MOQ, return policy) into the "Sections Flagged for Later" items below.
